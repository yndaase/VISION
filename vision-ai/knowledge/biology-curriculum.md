# Biology Curriculum Knowledge Base - WASSCE Preparation

## Overview
This knowledge base covers key biology topics for WASSCE (West African Senior School Certificate Examination) preparation, focusing on practical understanding and exam readiness.

## Section 1: Biology as the Science of Life

### Key Topics Covered:
- **Rhizopus (Fungus)**: Structure, life cycle, economic importance, saprophytic nutrition
- **Mosses (Bryophytes)**: Non-vascular plants, structure, reproduction, ecological roles
- **Ferns (Pteridophytes)**: Vascular plants, fronds, sporangia, life cycle
- **Agricultural Practices**: Soil preparation, seed sowing, fertilization, irrigation, pruning, grafting
- **Animal Husbandry**: Selective breeding, supplementary feeding, flushing, deworming

### Important Concepts:
- Photosynthesis and its role in crop production
- Reproduction methods in plants
- Nutrient cycling and soil fertility
- Biological pest control methods

## Section 2: Cytology (Cell Biology)

### Key Topics:
- **Cell Theory**: All living things are made of cells; cells are basic units of life; cells come from other cells
- **Cell Structures**: Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic cells, organelles and their functions
- **Specialized Cells**: Adaptations for specific functions (root hair cells, red blood cells, muscle cells, etc.)
- **Active Transport**: Movement against concentration gradient using ATP energy
- **Endocytosis & Exocytosis**: Bulk transport processes in cells

### DNA and Genetics:
- **DNA Structure**: Double helix, Watson-Crick model, nucleotides (A, T, G, C)
- **DNA Replication**: Process of copying genetic information before cell division
- **RNA**: Types (mRNA, tRNA, rRNA) and their roles in protein synthesis
- **Transcription**: DNA → RNA process
- **Translation**: RNA → Protein process
- **Genetic Code**: Codons and amino acids

## Section 3: Diversity of Living Things

### Insects Studied:
1. **Grain Weevil**: Pest of stored grains, complete metamorphosis, economic damage
2. **Butterfly**: Pollinator, complete metamorphosis (egg→larva→pupa→adult), ecological importance
3. **Housefly**: Disease vector, complete metamorphosis, sanitation importance
4. **Honeybee**: Pollinator, social insect, honey production, colony structure

### Habitats:
- **Tropical Rainforest**: High rainfall, biodiversity, layered canopy structure
- **Savannah**: Grasslands with scattered trees, seasonal rainfall (Guinea, Sudan, Sahel types)
- **Desert**: Low rainfall, extreme temperatures, specialized adaptations
- **Aquatic**: Lagoons, estuaries, rivers, lakes, ponds, seashore zones

### Adaptations:
- Plant adaptations to different environments
- Animal adaptations for survival
- Osmoregulation in aquatic organisms

### Health Topics:
- **Immunization**: Process of building immunity
- **Vaccination**: Administration of vaccines for disease prevention
- **Inoculation**: Historical method of inducing immunity

## Section 4: Systems of Life

### Cardiovascular System:
- **Heart Structure**: Four chambers (RA, LA, RV, LV), valves, septum
- **Blood Vessels**: Arteries, veins, capillaries - structure and function
- **Blood Components**: Plasma, RBCs, WBCs, platelets
- **Blood Pressure**: Normal vs hypertension (120/80 mmHg)

### Excretory System:
- **Kidneys**: Nephrons, ultrafiltration, reabsorption, urine formation
- **Skin**: Sweat production, temperature regulation
- **Lungs**: Gas exchange, CO₂ excretion
- **Liver**: Bile production, deamination, detoxification
- **Homeostasis**: Maintaining internal balance

### Plant Systems:
- **Xylem**: Water and mineral transport (transpiration stream)
- **Phloem**: Food transport (translocation)
- **Factors Affecting Transport**: Temperature, light, humidity, water availability

### Photosynthesis:
- **Equation**: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
- **Light-dependent reactions**: In thylakoid membranes, produces ATP and NADPH
- **Light-independent reactions (Calvin Cycle)**: In stroma, produces glucose
- **Limiting Factors**: Light intensity, CO₂ concentration, temperature, water, chlorophyll

## Study Tips for WASSCE:
1. Understand biological processes, don't just memorize
2. Practice drawing and labeling diagrams
3. Learn scientific terminology correctly
4. Relate concepts to real-life applications
5. Review past questions and exam patterns

## Common Exam Topics:
- Cell structure and function
- Photosynthesis and respiration
- Genetics and inheritance
- Human body systems
- Ecology and adaptations
- Agricultural biology
- Health and disease
